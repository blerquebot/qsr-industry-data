# Restaurant Site Selection Checklist

A practical checklist for QSR operators and franchisees evaluating potential locations. Site selection is one of the highest-impact decisions in restaurant operations, with location quality directly correlating to long-term revenue potential.

## Phase 1: Market Analysis

### Demographics
- [ ] Population within 3-mile radius (target: 20,000+ for suburban, 50,000+ for urban)
- [ ] Median household income aligns with brand positioning
- [ ] Daytime population (office workers, students) for lunch daypart
- [ ] Age distribution matches target customer profile
- [ ] Population growth trend (positive trajectory preferred)

### Competition
- [ ] Direct competitor count within 1-mile radius
- [ ] Complementary businesses nearby (retail, entertainment, grocery)
- [ ] Competitor performance assessment (busy vs. struggling locations)
- [ ] Market saturation analysis for your brand/category

### Economic Indicators
- [ ] Local employment rate and major employers
- [ ] New development projects (residential, commercial, infrastructure)
- [ ] Business climate and regulatory environment
- [ ] Property value trends

## Phase 2: Site-Specific Evaluation

### Access and Visibility
- [ ] Street frontage and signage visibility from main road
- [ ] Traffic count on adjacent roads (target: 20,000+ ADT for QSR)
- [ ] Speed of traffic (35-45 mph ideal for QSR awareness + ability to turn in)
- [ ] Ingress/egress quality (easy entry and exit from both directions)
- [ ] Traffic signal or median break for left turns
- [ ] Corner lot or end-cap position (preferred)

### Drive-Thru Specific (if applicable)
- [ ] Drive-thru lane stacking capacity (8+ cars minimum)
- [ ] Drive-thru exit does not conflict with parking lot traffic
- [ ] Menu board visibility and approach angle
- [ ] Dual-lane drive-thru feasibility
- [ ] Drive-thru does not back up onto public road

### Physical Site
- [ ] Lot size adequate for building, parking, drive-thru, landscaping
- [ ] Parking count meets code (typically 1 space per 3 seats + employees)
- [ ] Utility availability (gas, electric capacity, water, sewer, grease trap)
- [ ] Zoning allows restaurant use with drive-thru (if needed)
- [ ] Environmental assessment (Phase I minimum, Phase II if concerns)
- [ ] ADA compliance feasibility
- [ ] Delivery vehicle access (if applicable)

## Phase 3: Financial Evaluation

### Occupancy Costs
- [ ] Base rent per square foot (compare to market comps)
- [ ] CAM (Common Area Maintenance) charges
- [ ] Property tax pass-through or inclusion
- [ ] Insurance requirements
- [ ] Percentage rent clause (if applicable)
- [ ] Annual escalation rate (target: 2-3% or CPI-linked)
- [ ] Total occupancy below 10% of projected revenue

### Build-Out
- [ ] Estimated construction/renovation cost
- [ ] Landlord contribution (TI allowance)
- [ ] Timeline from lease signing to opening
- [ ] Permitting complexity and timeline
- [ ] Contractor availability in market

### Revenue Projection
- [ ] Comparable store volumes for similar locations
- [ ] Sales transfer analysis (if relocating or converting)
- [ ] Seasonality factors for the market
- [ ] Grand opening period estimate

## Phase 4: Lease Terms

- [ ] Lease term length (10-20 years typical for QSR)
- [ ] Renewal options (2-3 five-year options preferred)
- [ ] Exclusivity clause (no direct competitors in same center)
- [ ] Hours of operation flexibility
- [ ] Signage rights (monument, building, drive-thru)
- [ ] Assignment and subletting rights
- [ ] Co-tenancy clause (if in shopping center)
- [ ] Kick-out clause if sales targets not met
- [ ] Landlord maintenance responsibilities

## Red Flags

- Rent above 10% of realistic revenue projection
- No left-turn access from the primary traffic direction
- Limited parking relative to dining capacity
- Environmental concerns without resolution
- Excessive competition density with no differentiation angle
- Declining population or employment in the trade area
- Landlord restrictions on hours, signage, or drive-thru modifications
