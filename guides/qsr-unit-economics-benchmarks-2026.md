## 4-Wall EBITDA Benchmarks

4-wall EBITDA measures a single restaurant's operating profitability before corporate overhead, debt service, and capital expenditures. It is the most important unit-level metric for franchise buyers and operators evaluating site performance.

**How to calculate:** Revenue minus COGS, labor, occupancy, and controllable expenses at the store level.

| Metric | Top Quartile | Median | Bottom Quartile |
|--------|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| 4-Wall EBITDA Margin | 22-28% | 15-18% | 8-12% |
| Food Cost % | 26-28% | 30-33% | 35%+ |
| Labor Cost % | 25-28% | 30-33% | 35%+ |
| Occupancy Cost % | 6-8% | 9-11% | 13%+ |

**Why this matters:** The [42% of restaurant operators who lost money in 2025](https://qsr.pro/articles/restaurant-profitability-paradox-42-percent-unprofitable-record-revenue-2026) are overwhelmingly in the bottom quartile on 4-wall EBITDA. Record industry revenue of $1.55 trillion masks a profitability crisis at the unit level.

## Key Cost Pressures (2026)

**Labor:** Average restaurant hourly wage $19.68 (BLS, Jan 2026). Gap with all private-sector average ($37.32) is 47%. Industry turnover at 73.9% annually. [The labor paradox: 7.1% unemployment and operators still can't hire](https://qsr.pro/articles/restaurant-labor-paradox-7-percent-unemployment-operators-cant-hire-2026).

**Food Costs:** Running 35%+ above pre-pandemic levels. Beef at 75-year low cattle inventory. Eggs collapsed 42% YoY after avian flu recovery. Protein cost divergence is forcing menu engineering changes.

**Technology:** [73% of brands investing in AI, but only 5% report measurable ROI](https://qsr.pro/articles/restaurant-ai-investment-73-percent-adoption-only-5-percent-roi-qu-benchmark-2026). Tech spending averaging 3-5% of revenue for top chains.

**Occupancy:** Drive-thru site costs up 15-20% in top markets. [QSR real estate bidding wars intensifying](https://qsr.pro/articles/qsr-real-estate-bidding-war-drive-thru-sites-construction-costs-2026) as available sites shrink.
