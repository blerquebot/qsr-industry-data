## Summary

The U.S. restaurant industry is undergoing its most significant contraction since the pandemic. Across major chains alone, more than **1,000 locations** have been announced for closure in 2026. The closures are concentrated among the lowest-performing units in each system, driven by a convergence of declining consumer traffic, rising operating costs, and franchise economics that no longer support marginal locations.

For a full analysis of the forces driving this contraction, see: [The Great QSR Contraction: Nearly 1,000 Chain Restaurants Closing in 2026](https://qsr.pro/articles/the-great-qsr-contraction-nearly-1000-chain-restaurants-closing-in-2026)

## Chain-by-Chain Analysis

### Wendy's

- **Closures planned:** 298 to 358 U.S. locations in H1 2026
- **Q4 2025 U.S. same-store sales:** Down 11.3%
- **Full-year 2025 comps:** Down 5.6%
- **Key driver:** Management acknowledged the chain "swung the pendulum too far towards limited-time price promotions instead of everyday value"
- **Leadership status:** Interim CEO Ken Cook (CFO filling in); no permanent CEO named
- **2026 guidance:** Flat global systemwide sales; adjusted EBITDA $460M-$480M

Full analysis: [Wendy's Is Closing Up to 350 U.S. Locations. Here Is What Went Wrong.](https://qsr.pro/articles/wendys-closing-350-locations-turnaround-crisis-2026)

### Papa John's

- **Closures planned:** ~300 North American locations by end 2027; ~200 in 2026
- **Target profile:** Franchise-owned, 10+ years old, below $600,000 annual revenue
- **Q4 same-store sales:** Down 5.4% in North America
- **Turnaround plan:** CEO Todd Penegor's playbook focuses on cutting underperformers, kitchen operations, and rebuilding from a smaller base
- **Corporate cuts:** ~7% of corporate workforce eliminated

Full analysis: [Papa John's Is Closing 300 Restaurants. Here Is What the Turnaround Actually Looks Like](https://qsr.pro/articles/papa-johns-closing-300-locations-turnaround-strategy-2026)

### Pizza Hut

- **Closures planned:** ~250 U.S. locations in H1 2026
- **System trajectory:** Peaked at ~7,900 U.S. units in 2014; now below 6,500
- **Q4 2025 U.S. same-store sales:** Down 3%
- **Full-year 2025 comps:** Down 5%
- **Consecutive negative SSS quarters:** Seven
- **Strategic review:** Yum! Brands exploring whether Pizza Hut can reach "full potential" inside or outside the company (potential sale)

Full analysis: [Pizza Hut Is Closing 250 U.S. Locations in 2026. The Problem Runs Deeper Than You Think.](https://qsr.pro/articles/pizza-hut-closing-250-locations-yum-brands-strategy-2026)

### Jack in the Box

- **Closures planned:** 50-100 in fiscal 2026
- **Prior closures:** 200+ over fiscal 2024-2025
- **Q1 FY2026 same-store sales:** Down 6.7%
- **Total debt:** $1.7 billion; leverage at 6x net debt to adjusted EBITDA
- **Full-year FY2025 net loss:** $80.7 million
- **Turnaround plan:** CEO Lance Tucker's "Jack on Track" program

Full analysis: [Jack in the Box Enters Survival Mode: A 75-Year-Old Chain's Fight to Stay Relevant](https://qsr.pro/articles/jack-in-the-box-survival-mode-closures-turnaround-2026)

### Noodles & Company

- **Closures planned:** 30-35 (revised upward from initial 12-17)
- **2025 closures:** 42 locations (33 company-owned, 9 franchised)
- **Two-year contraction:** From 460+ to potentially 383 units (18%+ reduction)
- **Counterpoint:** Same-store sales up 6.6% systemwide in Q4; adjusted EBITDA up 32.7% in Q3

Full analysis: [Noodles & Company Is Doubling Its Planned Closures. Can It Stay Above 400 Locations?](https://qsr.pro/articles/noodles-company-doubling-closures-below-400-locations-pasta-turnaround-2026)

### Salad and Go

- **Closures completed:** ~75 locations (from 146 to ~71)
- **Markets exited:** Texas and Oklahoma entirely
- **Jobs cut:** ~600
- **Root cause:** CEO Mike Tattersfield found the company was "growing just for growth's sake"
- **Remaining footprint:** Arizona and Nevada only

Full analysis: [Salad and Go Cut Its Store Count in Half. The Turnaround Playbook Is a Lesson for Every Fast-Growing Chain.](https://qsr.pro/articles/salad-and-go-half-stores-closed-growth-discipline-tattersfield-2026)

- **Status:** Post-Chapter 11 bankruptcy (filed May 2024)
- **Current footprint:** ~550 locations (down from 700+)

### Long John Silver's

- **Current footprint:** 485 locations (down from ~1,500 at peak)
- **Three-year closures:** 154 units (49 in 2022, 71 in 2023, 34 in 2024)
- **Closure rate:** Roughly one location per week over three years
- **Structural challenge:** Seafood QSR carries uniquely high protein costs and limited daypart flexibility

- **Key issue:** Acquisition-fueled growth funded by stacked securitized debt

## Industry Context

According to Black Box Intelligence, the at-risk threshold for restaurant viability is defined as any location that has lost 30% or more of its peak sales. By BBI's 2026 estimates:

- **4%** of limited-service (QSR) restaurants meet that at-risk criteria
- **9%** of full-service restaurants meet that at-risk criteria
- **85-90%** of the restaurant industry remains resilient

The closures are concentrated in the weakest 10-15% of the operator base. The broader industry continues to grow: the global QSR market is projected to reach $868 billion by 2030, up from $659 billion in 2025.
