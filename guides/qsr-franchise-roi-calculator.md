# QSR Franchise ROI Calculator Framework

A structured approach to estimating return on investment for quick service restaurant franchise investments. This framework is designed for prospective franchisees evaluating single or multi-unit opportunities.

## Step 1: Total Investment Calculation

```
Total Investment = Initial Franchise Fee
                 + Real Estate (lease deposits, construction)
                 + Equipment and Fixtures
                 + Signage
                 + Opening Inventory
                 + Training Costs
                 + Working Capital (3-6 months recommended)
                 + Professional Fees (legal, accounting)
                 + Technology (POS, KDS, security)
                 + Miscellaneous/Contingency (5-10%)
```

**Typical ranges by brand tier:**
- Value tier (Subway, Little Caesars): $200K - $500K
- Mid-tier (Firehouse Subs, Jimmy John's): $300K - $800K
- Premium tier (McDonald's, Chick-fil-A): $1M - $3M+

For detailed cost breakdowns by brand, see: [QSR Pro Franchise Cost Center](https://qsr.pro/franchise-costs)

## Step 2: Revenue Projection

Use the brand's Item 19 (Financial Performance Representations) from the FDD as a starting point.

```
Projected Annual Revenue = AUV (Average Unit Volume) from FDD
Adjusted Revenue = AUV x Location Factor x Market Factor

Location Factors:
  - End cap with drive-thru: 1.0 - 1.15x
  - Inline strip mall: 0.85 - 0.95x
  - Non-traditional (airport, campus): varies widely

Market Factors:
  - Top 25 DMA: 1.0 - 1.1x
  - Secondary market: 0.9 - 1.0x
  - Rural: 0.75 - 0.9x
```

## Step 3: Profitability Model

```
Revenue                     100%
- COGS (food + packaging)   26-33%
- Labor (wages + burden)    25-33%
- Occupancy                  8-12%
- Royalty Fee                4-8%
- Ad Fund Contribution       2-5%
- Other Operating            5-8%
= Restaurant-Level EBITDA   10-20%

Healthy target: 15%+ restaurant-level EBITDA
Break-even point: typically 12-24 months
```

## Step 4: ROI Calculation

```
Simple ROI = Annual Net Profit / Total Investment x 100

Cash-on-Cash Return = Annual Cash Flow / Cash Invested x 100
(Accounts for financing - if you put $500K down on a $1.5M investment)

Payback Period = Total Investment / Annual Net Profit
Target: 3-5 years for most QSR investments

IRR (Internal Rate of Return) = discount rate that makes NPV = 0
Target: 15%+ for QSR franchise investments
```

## Step 5: Sensitivity Analysis

Model three scenarios:
1. **Conservative**: AUV at 25th percentile of FDD data, labor at high end
2. **Base case**: AUV at median, costs at median
3. **Optimistic**: AUV at 75th percentile, costs well-managed

## Red Flags in Franchise Economics

- Royalty + ad fund exceeding 10% of gross sales
- Occupancy costs above 12% in non-premium markets
- No Item 19 disclosure (franchisor won't share performance data)
- Franchisee turnover rate above 10% annually
- Required vendor programs with above-market pricing
